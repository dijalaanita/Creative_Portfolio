Project 1

Rendezvous

Rendezvous was an editorial fashion project created in collaboration with photographer and creative director Naren. The project explored visual storytelling through carefully curated styling, composition, and character-driven fashion narratives.

Overview

As the stylist, I was responsible for developing and executing the wardrobe direction for the shoot. I styled six complete looks, ensuring each outfit aligned with the project's creative vision while contributing to the overall visual narrative.

The styling process involved garment selection, outfit coordination, silhouette development, accessory pairing, and fitting sessions to refine each look before production. The final work was displayed as part of an art exhibition, showcasing the project as both a fashion editorial and a creative art piece.

Project 2

House of David

House of David was a fashion campaign created for WearJireh, a Christian fashion brand. The collection drew inspiration from the biblical story of King David, exploring themes of faith, courage, leadership, worship, and identity through contemporary fashion.

Overview

As the creative lead on the project, I oversaw the campaign from concept development through final execution. My responsibilities included creating the visual direction, styling the collection, planning and producing campaign imagery, capturing photo and video content, and managing the social media rollout surrounding the collection launch.

I styled five complete looks that reflected the collection's narrative while ensuring visual consistency across all campaign assets. The project required balancing creative storytelling with brand objectives to produce content that communicated the message of the collection while remaining visually compelling and marketable.
